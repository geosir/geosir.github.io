NOAA-on-a-Stick

Receiving Satellite Imagery with RTL-SDR

Several times a day, NOAA POES satellites pass overhead and transmit a live camera view of the ground below them. Using a cheap RTL-SDR dongle and a homemade antenna, I've been experimenting with receiving and decoding these signals to get pictures...from SPACE!

The project page contains a complete description of the project, as well as the latest received images.

George's New Schedule Machine

The Beautiful Course Schedule Visualizer

The Illinois Math and Science Academy uses PowerSchool for its Student Information System. One of its uses is to let students know what their course schedule will be for the following academic year. Unfortuneately, this exists as a indecipherable series of codes specifying days and periods--easy for a computer to understand, but hard for a student to visualize. My New Schedule Machine (much improved from the old one allows students to enter data from PowerSchool to generate colorful and organized bell schedules, making sharing schedulesa and finding classes on day one significantly easier.
