Club Hub

This is the personal project description page. To learn more about Club Hub, its features, and how it can help your school, visit clubhub.live.

Club Hub seeks to bring school announcements into the 21st century by uploading posters and events to the cloud. Through the magic of automation, it makes admnistrators' lives easier by automatically generating event listings and poster slideshows for digital displays.

Club Hub LIVE greets its viewers with a friendly title card.

I created Club Hub to help reduce information overload when I was a student at the Illinois Math and Science Academy. In my first year there, I immediately noticed that our campus-wide mailing list was spammy and annoying. Simultaneously, many students found it frustrating to keep track of events and posters when they were communicated through Facebook, which was not dedicated to organizing school events (since there is significant overlap with personal affairs). To put it shortly, communications at IMSA was heavily fragmented and confusing.

Although I began developing Club Hub as soon as I identified this need, the significant institutional inertia and early stage of the project made it difficult for me to convince students to use it. Still, seeing its great potential, I iteratively upgraded it whenever I got the time, hoping to build some critical feature that would kickstart its widespread adoption.

Then, disaster struck: a state budget crisis, cripping the monetary resources of our state-funded school. To plug expensive ink and paper costs from poster printing, the administration put a hold on all large-format poster prints out of our media department. This once-most-popular medium of club communication (students would collect these beautiful posters after events to decorate their rooms) was no longer available. As our inboxes exploded with ugly emails to compensate, it was clear that clubs and faculty needed a new way to communicate events.

The first Club Hub LIVE video ad, demonstrating is ability to host video posters.

That's when Club Hub sprung into the spotlight. Working with the school administration, I deployed three digital displays in high-traffic areas and worked to upgrade Club Hub to fit the administration's specifications. Since then, Club Hub has enjoyed immense success, and it has become the school's offical channel of club communication and event registration.

Now, I am working to develop Club Hub into a service available to any school that might benefit from it. To learn more about its features and pricing, visit clubhub.live.


A Brief History of Club Hub

A timeline showing Club Hub's various incarnations.

  • v0.0.1 beta (September 2014): Club Hub is born! It is very simple prototype— a mere set of static webpages each with a Google Calendar and a feed of emails for just that club.
  • v0.0.2 beta (Winter 2014): The first experiment with a "poster wall" and a management platform (Google Sheets, AppScript, and lots of JS).
  • v0.1.0 beta (Early 2015): Made a standalone poster wall. Beautified the admin sheet. Added a prototype auto-slideshow.
  • v0.2.0 beta: (Spring 2015): Experiments with different aesthetics to try to attract users, including a chalkboard aesthetic. It didn't work out, so I returned to minimalism.
  • v2.0.0 (Winter 2015): Inception of the event table. Added a feature to cast the slideshow on Chromecast. This is when I began collaborating with the school administration, putting Club Hub out of beta!
  • v3.0.1 (July 2016): The modern-day Club Hub, with new aesthetics, admin panel (so long, Sheets!), and robust presentation platform.
  • v3.1.0 (Spring and Summer 2017): Since Club Hub's success, I have overhauled Club Hub with bug fixes and efficiency improvements to ready it for use by all schools.
