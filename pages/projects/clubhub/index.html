Club Hub: See what's next in one glance.

Looking for IMSA Club Hub? Go to clubhub.live.

Club Hub is a framework designed to centralize and organize posters and event information into an easy-to-understand format, all through the magic automation of programming. It features a main event listing, an event reminder service, as well as a slideshow for digital displays.

Club Hub got its start at the Illinois Mathematics and Science Academy. The story goes like this:

As informative as it is, Students-l is widely regarded as spammy and annoying. At the same time, it's rather difficult to keep up IMSA Virtual Campus. Indeed, scrolling through an endless torrent of emails every day in addition to having to dedicate time to sort through a facebook page can be exhausting. With similar problems in a host of other channels, the result is a heavily fragmented communications scene on campus--it can be very difficult to get a clear idea of what's going on at IMSA.

Club Hub seeks to fix this by providing a location where you can visually grasp the order of events at IMSA. The idea is that if we use posters as "icons" for each event and arrange them in an intuitive way, it'll be easy to literally see what's happening at the academy. Thus our vision: to make it easy to see What the Next Big Thing at IMSA is.

Club Hub for Your Organization

Club Hub has recently been repackaged so that it can be downloaded and installed by anyone. It is released under the GPLv3 as open-source software.

The Goal

Club Hub is website that aims to make it easy to find out exactly what's up at IMSA in one swift glance. It seeks to complement the existing forms of communication--especially the mailing list and facebook page--by proving a resource that organizes communications beautifully in one place. In the end, Club Hub will help students answer the question, "What's the Next Big Thing at IMSA?"

The current sub-goals focus on the current minimum viable product provided by Club Hub: the poster wall. These goals are to 1) encourage the use of nice and clean posters as visual cues to the events/ideas/movements/etc. they represent; 2) provide organizational tools to students in order to help them better understand what the latest thing at IMSA is; and 3) streamline club and campus communications by consolidating the information all into one format at one accessible place.

As of August 2016, Club Hub has reached version 3.0.0 and found widespread popularity. It has been adopted as IMSA's official event management system. Still, there is room for improvement. Future updates to the system aim to include

  • A daily subscribeable email digest of events, replacing the large volume of club messages with a single letter
  • An event reminder system
  • Individual, easily managed club pages for each club that can be organized as to provide overview information
  • A sync-able Google Calendar of events

As Club Hub's popularity grows, so will its support base (I might train a team sometime). So stay tuned for updates!
